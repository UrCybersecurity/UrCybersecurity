## Could You Spot It?

Click each one to test yourself.

<details>
<summary><b>An email from "IT Support" asks you to verify your password by clicking a link. What do you do?</b></summary>
<br>
Don't click. Go directly to your company's IT portal or call the helpdesk using a number you already know. Legitimate IT requests almost never ask you to "verify" a password through email.
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>You get a voicemail that sounds exactly like your CEO, urgently requesting a wire transfer. Is it real?</b></summary>
<br>
Maybe not. AI voice cloning can convincingly fake a familiar voice from just seconds of audio. Always confirm unusual financial requests through a second channel before acting.
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>A text message says your package couldn't be delivered — click here to reschedule. Safe to click?</b></summary>
<br>
No. This is one of the most common phishing patterns (sometimes called "smishing"). Go directly to the carrier's official site or app instead of clicking the link.
</details>

This is the kind of real-world scenario covered in every UrCybersecurity training session.
